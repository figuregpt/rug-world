01 · The problem

NFTs used to connect people. Then it stopped.

NFT communities were the best place to meet people who shared your taste, your risk appetite, your internet. Real friendships formed. Real crews built real things. But the incentive model killed it.

Creators collected royalties on every secondary trade while the people holding, building the community, and keeping the project alive got nothing. When the hype faded, creators walked away with a recurring income stream. Holders were left with a JPEG and a dead Discord.

New communities stopped forming. Veteran collectors burned out. The door that NFTs opened for social connection quietly closed.

The old model in one line

Creator gets paid on every trade. Holder gets nothing. Community decays.

02 · The flip

What if royalties went to holders?

Rug.World reverses the royalty flow. 10% royalty on every secondary sale. All of it goes to people who stake their NFTs. Not the creator. Not the platform. The community.

The creator already earned from the mint. That is their payday. After that, the value that secondary trading generates belongs to the people who stayed.

Stakers receive 100% of the 10% royalty
Creator receives 0% royalty (earns from mint sales)

If a creator wants ongoing income from their own collection, they can mint NFTs for themselves and stake them. They earn like everyone else. Same rules, same skin in the game.

04 · Broken incentives

Holders used to hate high royalties. Now they want them.

Remember when 7.5% or 10% royalties felt like a tax? Holders complained because all that money went to the creator while they held the bag. They were right to be frustrated.

But when that same 10% goes to the people holding? It's not a tax anymore. It's income. The higher the royalty, the more holders earn. The conversation flips from "skip royalties" to "enforce them, they feed the pool I'm earning from."

Old model

High royalty = more money leaving the community into the creator's wallet. Traders avoid marketplaces that enforce royalties. Community shrinks.

Rug.World model

High royalty = more money flowing back to holders. Traders who pay royalties are feeding their own staking pool. Community grows.

05 · For creators

Mint price is your payday. And it's enough.

A 5,000-piece collection at 0.5 SOL is 2,500 SOL from mint alone. That's real money. Enough to build, enough to hire, enough to deliver.

If you want ongoing income, do what your holders do: keep some of your own NFTs and stake them. You earn proportionally from the same pool. You're not above the community. You're part of it. Same incentives, same upside, same skin in the game.

This changes the creator-holder relationship. You're not extracting from them. You're building alongside them. And that's the kind of project people actually want to stick with.

Creator who wants ongoing income

Pre-mint 500 NFTs to your wallet (free, only network fees). Stake all 500. Now you earn 10% of royalties (500/5000 = 10% of the pool) from every secondary trade. If your collection does well, you do well. If it doesn't, you don't get a free ride.

The best thing about the 2021-2022 NFT wave wasn't the art or the flips. It was the people. Communities that formed around collections created real friendships, real collaborations, real culture. Most of the people you know in web3 today? You probably met them through an NFT community.
